So, what are data breach alerts, and why do I need them?

The ADPCR Data Breach Alert service aims to notify Australian internet users of potential threats, while assisting in reducing risk for users who may be affected. This is a free service, operated by the Australian Data Privacy Certification Register.

Who can sign up for these alerts?

We don't discriminate. The information we provide within our data breach alerts is suitable for personal use, as well as business use - but our resources are primarily built to assist individuals. We already have hundreds of individual and corporate subscribers who take their privacy seriously.

Will these alerts only cover Australian data breaches?

Not exactly. We assesses each breach on a case-by-case basis - but if we have reason to believe that Australian internet users may be affected by a breach, it is possible we will activate an alert even if the company affected is based overseas.

How do you decide when to issue a data breach alert?

We take the activation of a breach alert very seriously. When assessing whether an alert should be activated, we look at a number of factors. These might include (but are not limited to) the location, the severity, and the size of the breach, as well as the likelihood of harm to our registered users. We will never exclude a breach from our alerts service due to commercial or political bias.

Hang on - how do you even know if a data breach has occured?

That's a good question. Australian businesses are pretty good at keeping their data breaches quiet. We aim to change that. We have a large network of contacts who provide us with information, which is also matched with information provided to us by the public. We always verify reports before issuing an alert, so you can rest assured that the information we are providing you is accurate.

Are you a Government agency?

No, and that's a good thing. We have more freedom to report on data breaches, and are not bound by dated legislation. This means we can provide you with the facts, faster.

All this talk of data breaches has scared me. How do I know I can trust you?

We pride ourselves on being pioneers in improving transparency, control, and disclosure in Australia. We will always tell you exactly what we plan to do with the information we collect. We've also built a world first Simplified Privacy Policy that explains, in plain language, exactly what we collect, why we collect it, and what rights you have to access, alter and delete it - at any time.

In the case of registering to this service, we promise we'll only use the data you provide to deliver data breach alerts. If you give us your name (or an alias), we'll only ever use that to address our emails to you. Your email address is securely stored and can only be accessed by the team that works across this service.

How do I opt-out of this service?

We're sad that you're even reading this, but we understand. If you don't think our alerts are useful to you, you can unsubscribe via the link at the bottom of every email we send you. Alternatively, you can just click here & enter the email address you registered with.
